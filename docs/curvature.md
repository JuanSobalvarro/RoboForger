To be able to approximate curves we use a chord error which derives from the sagitta definition, when we minimize the sagitta to a certain value we can guarantee that the curve will be approximated with a certain precision. The sagitta is defined as the distance from the midpoint of the chord to the arc of the curve, and it can be calculated using the formula:
s = r - sqrt(r^2 - (c/2)^2)

Where:
- s is the sagitta
- r is the radius of the curve
- c is the length of the chord
- sqrt is the square root function
- /2 is the division by 2 to get the half of the chord length
- - is the subtraction to get the distance from the midpoint of the chord to the arc of the curve
- The chord error is defined as the maximum distance between the curve and the chord, and it can be calculated using the formula:
- e = s / r

Where:
- e is the chord error
- s is the sagitta
- r is the radius of the curve
- / is the division to get the ratio of the sagitta to the radius of the curve

By minimizing the chord error to a certain value, we can guarantee that the curve will be approximated with a certain precision. The smaller the chord error, the closer the approximation will be to the actual curve.
